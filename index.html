Philosophy / Statement of Career Goal

I am motivated by work that solves real problems for real people, whether that means clarifying a messy workflow, reducing friction in how teams share knowledge, or helping stakeholders see a path from idea to implementation. I believe the strongest solutions emerge when business context and technology are treated as one conversation, not two separate languages. That is why I invest in both structured analysis and hands-on technical practice: it keeps my recommendations grounded and my collaboration with developers respectful and efficient.

I value continuous learning, user-centered thinking, and honest iteration based on feedback. Academic projects, leadership roles, and hackathon experiences have reinforced that clear requirements, empathy for users, and disciplined communication are just as important as any framework or tool. My goal is to grow into a Business Analyst or product-oriented professional who helps organizations make better decisions, supported by evidence, aligned with stakeholders, and designed for sustainable delivery.
